# Screen Quality Checklist

- Is the primary action obvious within 5 seconds?
- Are `loading`, `empty`, `error`, and `success` states designed?
- Does the layout adapt intentionally across desktop, tablet, and mobile?
- Are touch, pointer, and keyboard needs covered where relevant?
- Does the screen feel trustworthy and emotionally steady during errors and
  recovery?
- Is hierarchy created through spacing, type, and grouping before extra color?
- Would this still feel good during repeated daily conversations?
